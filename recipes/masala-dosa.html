Masala Dosa

Description

Embark on a culinary journey to South India with the legendary Masala Dosa. This iconic dish combines a beautifully golden, fermented rice-and-lentil crepe with a fragrant, turmeric-infused potato mash called 'aloo masala'. Traditionally pan-roasted with rich ghee or oil, it offers a distinct tangy flavor from its natural fermentation process. Served piping hot alongside a bowl of aromatic sambar (lentil stew) and creamy coconut chutney, it is the ultimate comfort food for breakfast or brunch.

Ingredients

For the Crispy Dosa Batter:

  • Idli Rice / Parboiled Rice: 1.5 cups (provides the characteristic texture)
  • Raw Rice: ½ cup
  • Urad Dal (Split White Lentils): ½ cup
  • Chana Dal (Bengal Gram): 1 tablespoon (helps get a golden colour)
  • Fenugreek Seeds (Methi): ½ teaspoon (aids fermentation and aroma)
  • Poha (Flattened Rice): ¼ cup (keeps the dosa light and crisp)
  • Salt: 1 teaspoon (or to taste)
  • Ghee or Neutral Oil: For frying the crepes

For the Potato Masala (Filling):

  • Potatoes: 4 medium, boiled, peeled, and gently mashed
  • Onions: 1 large, thinly sliced
  • Green Chillies: 2, finely chopped
  • Ginger: 1 teaspoon, finely grated
  • Curry Leaves: 1 sprig (around 8–10 leaves)
  • Mustard Seeds: ½ teaspoon
  • Chana Dal: 1 teaspoon (soaked in warm water for 10 minutes)
  • Turmeric Powder: ½ teaspoon
  • Asafoetida (Hing): A pinch
  • Fresh Coriander (Cilantro): 2 tablespoons, chopped (for garnish)
  • Salt: To taste

Steps

Prepare and Ferment the Batter:

  1. Soak: Wash the rice and poha together in one bowl. In a separate bowl, rinse the urad dal, chana dal, and fenugreek seeds. Soak both bowls in ample water for 5 hours.
  2. Grind: Drain the lentils and grind them with a little cold water until light, fluffy, and smooth. Next, grind the rice mix into a smooth, slightly grainy batter.
  3. Combine: Mix both batters thoroughly in a large deep bowl. Add salt, cover with a loose lid, and let it ferment in a warm place for 8 to 10 hours until it rises and becomes frothy.

Make the Potato Masala Filling:

  1. Temper: Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan. Add the mustard seeds and let them crackle. Add the soaked chana dal and saute until goldenbrown.
  2. Aromatics: Throw in the sliced onions, green chillies, grated ginger, and curry leaves. Sauté until the onions turn soft and translucent.
  3. Season: Stir in the turmeric powder, asafoetida, and salt. Pour in ¼ cup of water and let it simmer for a minute.
  4. Finish: Add the mashed potatoes, mix well, and cook for 3 minutes on low heat until the mixture thickens slightly. Garnish with fresh coriander and set aside.

Assemble and Cook the Dosa:

  1. Heat the Pan: Bring a non-stick or cast-iron tawa (griddle) to medium heat. Splash a few drops of water on it (it should sizzle away immediately) and wipe it clean with a kitchen paper towel or cloth.
  2. Spread the Batter: Pour a ladleful of fermented batter exactly into the center of the pan. Working quickly, use the back of the ladle to swirl the batter outward in an even, circular spiral motion to make a thin crepe.
  3. Roast: Drizzle half a teaspoon of ghee or oil over the surface and around the edges of the dosa. Cook on a medium flame until the base turns deep golden brown and the edges lift naturally.
  4. Stuff & Fold: Place 2–3 tablespoons of the prepared potato masala right in the center of the dosa. Fold the sides of the crepe over the filling and gently lift it off the griddle.
  5. Serve: Transfer immediately to a plate and serve hot with fresh coconut chutney and spicy vegetable sambar.
